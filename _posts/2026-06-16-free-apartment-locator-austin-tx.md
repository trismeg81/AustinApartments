## What Does an Apartment Locator Actually Do?

Think of me as a buyer's agent, but for renters. You tell me what you need — budget, neighborhood, move-in date, must-haves — and I do the legwork: pulling up-to-date availability, comparing floor plans and pricing, flagging current specials, and making sure you're not wasting time on places that won't work.

I know which communities have solid management, which ones are running deals right now, and which ones to steer clear of. That local knowledge is hard to get from a listing site.

Once I narrow it down, I set up your tours, come with you if you want, and help you make sense of the lease before you sign anything. You get a real person in your corner — not an algorithm.

## Why Is It Free for Renters?

This is the question I get most. Short answer: apartment communities pay me a referral fee when you sign a lease. It comes out of their marketing budget — the same budget they'd spend on advertising on Zillow or Apartments.com. You never see a bill from me, and there's nothing added to your rent.

This model has been standard in the apartment industry for a long time, and it works because everyone wins. You get personalized help at no cost. The community gets a qualified renter. I get paid for connecting the two.

Your rent is the same whether you found the apartment yourself or came through me. Often it's actually lower — because I know about specials that aren't posted publicly.

## How I Find Deals You Won't See Online

Listing sites show you what communities want you to see. What they don't always show: the negotiated specials that get offered to locators and their clients.

Right now in Austin, those deals often look like:

- **Weeks of free rent** — sometimes 6–8 weeks on longer lease terms
- **Waived application and admin fees**, which can run $200–$400 at move-in
- **Reduced deposits** for qualified applicants
- **Access to units before they hit the general market**

I'm in contact with leasing teams across Austin regularly. When a community has open units or needs to hit an occupancy goal, locators hear about it first. That's how I bring you deals you genuinely can't find scrolling Apartments.com at midnight.
